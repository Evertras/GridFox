About Gridify It

So I did! I used this as an opportunity to learn more AngularJS along with fancy new HTML5 features, so it's a win/win as it's keeping my skills sharp and improves my front end development, which is a little rusty.

Why did I share this?

I like making stuff that other people can use. It makes me really happy seeing people get use out of this! I also really appreciate feedback and criticism to improve, as this is related to my job and doing this as a side project helps make me better at my job.

Where do my images go? How does this work?

Nowhere. Nothing is uploaded to the server. I can't see what you're importing because it's never sent anywhere. It's all local in your browser! I do this mostly for privacy concerns, and partially because images flying all over the internet can start eating up bandwidth that isn't necessary to use. It's a win for everyone. Everything is saved locally as well, so you should be able to pick up right where you left off if you close your browser. Just remember it won't transfer to another device!

How does the height and width work?

You probably noticed that there's no 'inches' or 'centimeters' with the height and width. This is because these are just ratios to help size the canvas. If you have a canvas that's 18" by 24", for example, just put in 18 and 24. If it's 30 centimeters by 30 centimeters, just put in 30 and 30. Since the canvas has to resize itself to fit the page anyway, there's no point in using specific units. It's only to figure out the aspect ratio and help plan out your piece.

What's the 'phi' grid?

Phi is a grid based on the golden ratio and is thought to be more pleasing to the eye than the more general 'rule of thirds' in some cases. Play around with it and make your own judgements with your compositions.

Does this work for tablets?

You know what would be great? Being able to upload your images from your smart phone or tablet and doing stuff from there. You know what would be even more awesome? If this actually worked right now. I'm looking into it, but in the meantime there are a fair amount of native apps out there for iOS and Android that might work better for you. This is mainly targeted to desktop/laptop users for now.
